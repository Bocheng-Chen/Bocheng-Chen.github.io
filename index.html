My name is Bocheng Chen and I'm a tenure-track Assistant Professor of Computer and Information Science at the University of Mississippi. I study computer security, with a focus on risks in AI-based systems. My research aim to identify and mitigate realistic security vulnerabilities in LLM-based chatbot systems to ensure their secure operation.

[Prospective PhD Students and Interns] My research lab, Secured AI Systems (SAIS) Lab, plans to recruit 3 Ph.D. students interested in AI for Security in Spring and Fall 2026. Interested applicants may contact me at cbchen7[at]gmail[dot]com with a CV and transcript, using the email subject: [26Fall/26Spring-PhDApplication-Name].

My research aims to address the following question: What is the worst-case behavior when chatbots are widely deployed in real-world settings, and can we design secure chatbot systems given that current large language models, due to their probabilistic nature, cannot reliably enforce security policies?

I am extremely fortunate to collaborate closely with Guangliang Liu at Michigan State University and Dr. Zoe Xi Chen at Nanyang Technological University.
